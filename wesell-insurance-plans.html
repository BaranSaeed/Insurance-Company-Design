Types of Auto Insurance Coverage:

Liability Insurance

Liability coverage pays for damages your auto causes to others or their personal property. Each state has different liability limits drivers must meet in order to operate a vehicle.

Comprehensive Coverage

Comprehensive coverage pays for damages to your (the insureds) vehicle in the event of fire, theft, vandalism or collision with a deer or bird. It does not cover loss due to a collision with another vehicle.

Collision Coverage

Collision insurance coverage pays for any physical damage done to your vehicle caused by another vehicle or stationary object. Such as; fence, telephone pole, building, etc.

Medical Payments Coverage

Medical payments coverage pays for medical expenses (up to a limit) or death benefits to anyone covered under your policy in the event of an auto accident. This coverage is paid regardless of fault.

Underinsured Motorist Bodily Injury Coverage

Underinsured motorist bodily injury coverage pays for injuries or medical expenses to you in the event of an accident when the responsible party (other than you) does not have adequate auto insurance coverage.

Uninsured Motorist Bodily Injury Coverage

Uninsured motorist bodily injury coverage pays for injuries or medical expenses when your injured in an auto accident with a person who has no insurance or can’t be located.

Road Service Coverage

Road Service Coverage pays for (up to a limit) you to have roadside assistance in the event that your auto breaks down. This would include towing and labor costs at the site the car is broken down.

Personal Injury Protection (PIP)

Personal Injury protection covers expenses related to injuries incurred in a covered accident. Types of expenses it can include: medical, hospital bills not covered by your health insurance, loss of services, funeral expenses, income continuation. PIP coverage will vary depending on the state you live in.


How to Save Money on Auto Insurance

You could save hundreds on your auto insurance by seeing if you are eligible for insurance discounts. Auto insurance discounts bring down your premium but not your coverage. Your car’s safety features could also help reduce your premium. Examples of Auto Insurance discounts are:

Passive Restraint Discount

If your vehicle is equipped with airbags and motorized seatbelts, you could save $$.

Anti-Lock Brake discount

Most insurance companies give discounts for anti lock brakes.

Economy Car Discounts

Receive a discount when you own an economy “gas friendly” car.

Safe Drivers Discounts

Your driving habits can save you hundreds. By being a safe driver with tickets or accidents you are rewarded with lower insurance rates.
